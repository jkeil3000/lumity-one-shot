# Lumity — Product Notes & Roadmap Items
*Captured from strategic session — March 2026*

---

## Core Product Philosophy (Reaffirmed)
- Personal tracking is the FOUNDATION of the social layer, not a separate feature
- Someone should be able to use and enjoy the app without any community (solo use case must be strong)
- As their public collections/playlists grow, their social identity emerges naturally
- **Library is private** — personal tracking only, not visible to others
- **Public playlists/collections** are what get displayed on profiles and shared socially — user controls what crosses that line
- This privacy distinction is core to the product trust model
- Profile = a place that reflects how you think, not a timeline of activity
- Curation > chronology. Taste > activity.

---

## ICP (Ideal Community Profile)
Not a demographic — an identity and worldview:
- Politically thoughtful, not partisan. Politically homeless.
- Strong beliefs loosely held
- Tired of noise, polarization, performative content
- Wants to think for themselves
- Engages with disagreement with curiosity, not hostility
- Cares about society but isn't consumed by any movement or creed
- Wants to protect their attention and inputs intentionally
- Believes in the advancement of humanity, community, real conversation

**Key insight:** Market to the worldview, not the demographic. Describe it so precisely they feel seen and self-select in.

---

## Cultural North Star
**"A home for people who think for themselves."**

Everything — copy, onboarding, community guidelines, content, design — should ladder up to this.

---

## Language / Framing
- Users → Community members or Curators
- "Follow" → "Join X's community"
- Avoid vanity metric language throughout
- Empty states should carry the platform's voice and worldview

## Product Roadmap Items

### 🔴 High Priority

**1. Onboarding Redesign**
- Current problem: Users hit the feed immediately, think it's purely social, have no one to interact with, and churn
- Fix: Onboarding flow should narrate the personal → social arc
- Use micro-copy to orient users at each step:
  - Library setup: "This is yours. Private, personal, just for you — your own record of everything you've consumed."
  - Public collections setup: "This is how people will know you. Share only what you want the world to see."
  - First feed view: "This is your community's thinking. Add to it and it gets richer."
- Flow first, explainers only when needed
- Include a **Founder's Note** during onboarding (see below)

**2. Empty States Overhaul**
- Current state: Generic, uninviting, miss the brand voice entirely
- Fix: Every empty state should carry Lumity's worldview
- Example: Instead of "Add your first book" → "The best media diet starts with one honest recommendation."
- Treat empty states as the first impression of the culture

**3. Founder's Note — Onboarding**
- A brief, personal note from JJ during onboarding
- NOT a formal welcome letter — his actual voice
- Should explain why he built this and who it's for
- Tone reference: the "I'm building Lumity because..." stream of consciousness from this session
- Sets cultural tone before someone sees a single piece of content

### 🟡 Medium Priority

**4. Curator Identity System**
- Replace vanity metrics with depth indicators
  - Not "124 followers" but "47 recommendations across 6 categories"
  - Communicates range and commitment, not popularity
- Endorsement mechanic: curator-to-curator endorsements on *profiles* (not just individual pieces of content) — more meaningful than upvotes, signals overall trust in someone's taste
- Profile richness as visual signal — a well-curated profile should *look* noticeably richer than a sparse one through design, not badges. A well-furnished room looks different from an empty one.
- **Trusted Curator** designation:
  - Granted by internal moderation panel (team votes, not algorithm)
  - Based on tracked internal metrics — consistency, depth, diversity, engagement quality
  - Panel approach: democratic but not authoritarian — set clear criteria, vote collectively
  - Should feel like a genuine mark of community respect, not a blue check

**5. Topic Architecture — Broad Not Algorithmic**
- All perspectives on a topic grouped together (e.g. all political content together, not siloed by ideology)
- Goal: expose users to range, encourage engagement across disagreement
- Resist the urge to personalize into an echo chamber

**6. Community Guidelines as Cultural Document**
- Guidelines shouldn't feel like legal terms
- Should read like a manifesto of the community's values
- Debate ideas, not people. Curiosity over hostility. Strong beliefs loosely held.

### 🟢 Future / Longer Term

**7. Full Product Roadmap Document**
- Needs to be built out formally
- Should map features to the core philosophy and ICP
- Prioritize by: solo use case strength → social layer depth → curator economy visibility

**8. Discourse / Debate Infrastructure**
- Current gap: product supports personal consumption and like-minded connection
- Vision requires infrastructure for respectful cross-perspective engagement
- This is what separates Lumity from "better Goodreads" and makes it a movement
